# One-Pot Thai Peanut Chicken Curry

Curries are notoriously high in calories because of full-fat coconut milk and peanut butter. You can hack this for a deficit by using light coconut milk and powdered peanut butter (which removes the fat but keeps the roasted peanut flavor and adds extra protein).

## Equipment
- 1 large pot or Dutch oven

## Yields
4-5 servings

## Ingredients
- 1 kg chicken breast, diced
- 1 can (400ml) light coconut milk
- 3-4 tbsp Thai red curry paste
- 4 tbsp powdered peanut butter (like PB2)
- 1 large bag of frozen stir-fry vegetables (broccoli, carrots, snap peas)
- 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional, but adds great umami)

## Instructions
1. Place the pot over medium heat. Sauté the chicken until lightly browned on the outside.
2. Add the red curry paste and stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in the light coconut milk, powdered peanut butter, and fish sauce. Whisk until smooth.
4. Dump in the frozen vegetables, bring to a gentle bubble, and let it simmer uncovered for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens and the chicken is cooked through.
